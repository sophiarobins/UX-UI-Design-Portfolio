01 — The Problem

The market
wasn't the issue.
The signal was.

The wedding photography market is saturated. Every photographer has a website. Most of them look the same — a slider of golden-hour photos, some pricing, a contact form. They compete on similarity.

I wasn't trying to be another option. I was trying to become the obvious choice for couples who were already willing to invest in quality. That required a website that didn't just show work — it communicated a standard of care that most photographers never bother to design for.

"High-end couples don't just hire a photographer. They hire the entire experience they imagine they'll have. The website is the first chapter of that story — and most photographers let WordPress write it for them."

02 — Process

Strategy before
a single pixel.

Before writing a line of code, I mapped out what the site needed to accomplish across four distinct phases — and what failure would look like at each one.

Phase 01

Brand Positioning

Defined the ideal client profile, competitive whitespace, and the emotional experience the site needed to deliver. Forest green, cream, and gold weren't decoration — they were a positioning statement.

Phase 02

Information Architecture

Mapped the full conversion path: discover → trust → desire → inquire. Every page, every section, every CTA was placed to move a visitor forward — not just inform them.

Phase 03

Design & Build

Designed in the browser using WordPress + custom HTML/CSS. Full mobile-first build. Typography, spacing, and image treatment were all done by hand — no theme drag-and-drop.

Phase 04

SEO & Launch

Schema markup, meta strategy, image optimization, and page speed tuning. Built to rank for local wedding photography keywords from day one.

03 — What I Built

Six systems.
One cohesive
experience.

Each feature of the website was designed to solve a specific problem in the client acquisition journey — from the first impression to the moment they hit send on an inquiry form.

01

Custom Brand Identity

Visual Language · Typography · Colour

Forest green, cream, and gold were chosen to communicate three things simultaneously: trustworthiness, warmth, and premium quality. Cormorant Garamond sets an editorial tone that immediately signals fine-art photography — not family portraits. Every font pairing, colour hex, and spacing decision was made to attract a client who is already spending premium and needs a visual reason to stop scrolling.

02

Portfolio & Gallery

Curation · Performance · Layout

The gallery was built with deliberate curation over volume. Ten extraordinary images outperform sixty average ones. The layout uses asymmetric masonry with controlled white space — designed to feel like a curated editorial spread, not a grid of thumbnails. Images are lazy-loaded and WebP-optimised to maintain speed without sacrificing quality.

03

Booking & Inquiry Flow

Conversion · UX · Form Design

The inquiry form was designed to pre-qualify leads, not just collect emails. Fields capture date, venue, budget range, and how they found the site — giving every inquiry enough context to respond personally within minutes. The CTA copy was tested against three alternatives; "Inquire About Your Date" consistently outperformed "Contact Me" and "Get in Touch" for conversion intent.

04

Pricing Page

Transparency · Anchoring · Trust

Most photographers hide their pricing and lose half their leads to uncertainty. The pricing page was designed with transparency as a brand signal — "we're confident enough in our value to show you what it costs." Package anchoring positions the mid-tier as the clear choice. The layout guides the eye naturally toward the most popular option without using manipulative dark patterns.

05

Blog & Journal

SEO · Storytelling · Long-tail

The blog section serves two masters: SEO and story. Each post targets a specific long-tail keyword (venue names, "outdoor wedding photography," seasonal terms) while telling the story of a real wedding. This converts organic traffic into trust — a visitor who reads a full wedding story is significantly warmer than one who just browsed the gallery. Schema markup ensures posts appear as rich results in search.

06

SEO Architecture

Technical SEO · Schema · Page Speed

Built with a full SEO strategy from the ground up: semantic HTML structure, custom meta titles and descriptions per page, JSON-LD schema for local business and creative works, image alt text strategy, internal linking architecture, and a sitemap. Page speed targets above 90 on mobile via image compression, defer-loaded scripts, and minimal plugin dependency.

05 — Design Decisions

Nothing was
accidental.

The choices that define this site aren't aesthetic preferences — they're strategic decisions made in service of one outcome: booking more of the right couples.

🎨

Colour as Positioning

Forest green signals growth, trust, and organic luxury. Cream is warm and welcoming — not cold or clinical. Gold marks the premium tier without shouting it. Together, they define a market position without a single word of copy.

"Most photographers choose colours they personally like. I chose colours my ideal client would instinctively trust."
✍️

Typography as Tone

Cormorant Garamond carries the weight of editorial publishing — Vogue, Town & Country, fine-art print. Pairing it with DM Sans keeps the UI readable without sacrificing the luxe signal. The combination says: this is beautiful, and it works.

"Font choices are brand choices. I wanted a couple to feel the quality before they'd read a single word."
📱

Mobile as Primary

87% of couples discover photographers on their phones — browsing late at night, sharing links with partners. The entire site was designed mobile-first, with desktop as the graceful expansion. No elements that break on small screens. No text that's unreadable at 375px.

"Designing mobile-first isn't a trend. It's designing for where your actual audience actually is."
💰

Transparent Pricing

Hiding pricing protects nothing and loses everyone who won't fill out a contact form just to find out if they can afford you. Showing pricing filters out bad-fit leads and signals confidence. It's a brand decision dressed as a business one.

"The couples who are right for me aren't scared off by a number. They're qualified by it."
🔍

SEO from Day One

SEO wasn't retrofitted — it was baked into the information architecture. Page titles, URL slugs, heading hierarchy, schema markup, and blog strategy were all planned before a single page was built. Organic traffic is the highest-intent traffic; it was worth building for.

"A beautiful website nobody finds is a portfolio piece. A beautiful website that ranks is a booking engine."
💬

Copy as Conversion

Every line of copy was written to a specific job: establish desire, build trust, overcome an objection, or prompt an action. Nothing is filler. The hero headline, about page, and CTA copy were all tested against alternatives. Copywriting is design.

"What the page says is inseparable from how it looks. I treated every word as a design decision."
06 — SEO & Conversion Strategy

Built to be
found and
remembered.

Technical SEO

  • ✦Semantic HTML5 structure with correct heading hierarchy throughout
  • ✦Custom meta titles and descriptions written per page, not auto-generated
  • ✦JSON-LD schema markup for LocalBusiness and CreativeWork types
  • ✦WebP image format with explicit width/height to prevent layout shift
  • ✦XML sitemap and robots.txt configured at launch
  • ✦Core Web Vitals optimized: LCP, CLS, and FID all in green

Conversion Architecture

  • ✦CTA placement mapped to natural reading flow, not just "above the fold"
  • ✦Inquiry form fields designed to pre-qualify, not just collect contact info
  • ✦Pricing anchoring: three tiers, one obvious choice
  • ✦Blog content targets long-tail venue and location keywords
  • ✦Social proof positioned at every decision point, not just one testimonials page
  • ✦Zero third-party redirects in the inquiry flow — no tool-switching friction
